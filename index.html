Explore Novels Fast

Knowing when to fluctuate the emotional tone of your novel can be a key factor in keeping your reader turning the page. One of the best ways to learn how to do this is by studying a variety of novels.

How to find time to study numerous novels?

How do you find time to not just read novels but study them when you are spending your free time writing?

Easily explore the negative dips and positive climbs of six classical novels, without having to read every one from start to finish. Each sentence in the novel has been given a score between -1 and 1. Negative one being the most negative and 1 being the most positive. The novels are broken up into twenty pieces so that they can be equally compared via bar charts. The height of each bar in the chart is determined by the mean of the sentiment scores of that section of the novel.

Click on one of the bars in the charts to read the sentencess represented by that bar and get a feel for how the novel's emotional tone fluctuates through out the story. The bar charts represent the novels in chronological order.

How is the score calculated?

Sentiment analysis was used to determine the tone variations of the novels. Sentiment analysis, uses statistical models, to try and gage the negative and positive intention behind a series of words, often used to gain an understanding of attitudes, opinions, and emotions expressed online. The python library TextBlob was used in this project.
