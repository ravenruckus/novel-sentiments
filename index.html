Explore Novels Fast

Knowing when to fluctuate the emotional tone of your novel can be a key factor in keeping your reader turning the page. One of the best ways to learn how to do this is by studying a variety of novels. How do you find time to not just read novels but study them when you are spending your free-time writing?

Easily explore the negative dips and positive climbs of six classical novels, without having to read every one from start to finish. Each sentence in the novel has been given a score between -1 and 1. Negative one being the most negative and 1 being the most positive. The novels were then borken up into twenty pieces and the mean score of the sentence sentiment scores calculated so that you can see the pattern each novel makes over the course of its story. Click one of the bars in the charts below to read the mean sentiment score and the sentences that comprise that section of the novel.

How is the score calculated?

Sentiment analysis was used to determine the tone variations of the novels. Sentiment analysis, uses statistical models, to try and gage the negative and posistive blah blah behind a series of words, often used to gain an understanding of attitudes, opinions, and emotions expressed online. The python library TextBlob was used in this project.
